The project

IAMANEH (International Association for Maternal and Neonatal Health) was founded in 1978 by the Swiss Gynecologist Hubert de Watteville and is a ZEWO certified charity. It focuses on the physical, social and psychological well-being of women and children in Mali, Senegal, Togo, Burkina Faso, Bosnia-Herzegovina and Albania. The "Token of Gratitude" community fundraiser supports a 3-year project (2016-2019) in Burkina Faso, which IAMANEH have implemented in collaboration with their local partner AMMIE (Association pour l'Appui Moral, Matériel et Intellectuel de l'Enfant). Their focus is to provide young women with information and access to reliable means of family planning. Teenage pregnancies are often the start of a vicious circle, leading to family rejection, early school leaving, and eventually to prostitution and other dependencies. In cooperation with AMMIE, IAMANEH provides schooling for “peer-educators”, enabling more than 100 young women and men to engage with their local communities about issues related to family planning. They also run health centers, helping young women to access information, support and contraceptive means. Additionally, IAMANEH supports young women with children in building up their own source of income, such as a sewing or barber shop, helping them to achieve financial independence.

Our cause

As part of our commitment to equal opportunities for everyone, Trust Square together with Procivis and FDP. The Liberal Women Switzerland launched a community fundraiser to benefit young women in Ouahigouya, Burkina Faso. For this purpose, we have built a charity smart contract called "Token of Gratitude", based on which individuals can contribute to the project by donating Ether. In return, the smart contract will issue a token to the contributor's wallet, serving as a "donation certificate". Attached to the token there is a virtual flower, created randomly for each donor. The flower can be viewed via the "view donation" function on this website.

The smart contract will run for a total of 8 weeks (until August 7, 2018) during which it will allocate all contributions to the wallet of the charity. You can find the repository of the smart contract here.

We believe that fundraising via public ledger can greatly increase transparency and reduce administrative costs for charitable giving. We are very happy that the Swiss charity IAMANEH agreed to collaborate with us to accept donations denominated in Ether.
